About

I did my undergraduate at Imperial College London, where biochemistry meant spending a lot of time with protein structures, mass spectrometers, and R scripts. My final-year project looked at the CHKB Q328R variant using cryo-EM, NMR, and PyMOL.

Now I'm at IE University in Madrid, doing a dual degree in Management and Computer Science & Business Technology. The pairing is deliberate: I want to work in roles where you need both the quantitative depth and the ability to communicate it to people who didn't do a science degree.

Outside class I build things: a crisis-navigation platform that won 2nd at a hackathon, a seq2seq transformer for chemical name translation, ML toxicity models, and quant finance pipelines. I'm most interested in pharma tech, fintech, and biotech roles in Spain.
